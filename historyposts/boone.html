The Daniel Boone Homestead

In 1730, Squire Boone purchases 250 acres of land and erects a log house and spring cellar. By 1750, the Boone family moves to North Caroline and William Maugridge purchases 159 acres of the Boone Property.

Around 1750, the right-hand section of the stone house is constructed on the foundation of the Boone's log cabin and spring house. In 1770, John DeTurk purchases the Boone's land after William Maugridge's death in 1766.

The DeTurk family is of French Huguenot descent and is descendant from Isaac DeTurk who immigrated to the Hudson River valley in New York in 1708. Isaac was from Picardy in northern France. In 1685, the Revocation of the Edict of Nantes occurred prompting Huguenots throughout France and Alsace-Lorraine to begin fleeing into areas such as England, Holland, Switzerland, Germany, Sweden, Italy, and even Ireland. After the Protestant persecution in France, Isaac opted to flee to America after living for a time in the Alsace-Lorraine region of Germany. The DeTurks were among the many Huguenot refugees who eventually made their way to America. In 1712, Isaac came to the Oley Valley from New York.
